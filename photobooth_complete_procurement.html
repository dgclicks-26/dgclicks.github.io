Why two tubes spliced: a single TL60 at 75cm only covers roughly 80cm of horizontal spread at 1.5m distance — not enough for a group of 6–8. What changed
Items removed from original list
Two items from the original equipment list are removed entirely in the updated spec. One is replaced, one is eliminated.
Removed — do not buy
2× Godox SL60W LED lights + 80cm octabox softboxes + stands
Previously recommended as freestanding studio lights on extending arms
S$280–320
Why removed: The SL60W is a cylindrical lamp head requiring a 25cm-deep cavity when paired with a softbox. The wing fin cavity is only 8cm deep. Additionally, the SL60W has an active cooling fan that exhausts heat rearward — this fan would be blocked and damaged inside a sealed fin. Flat LED panels (Viltrox VL-60B) are the correct replacement — same light quality, ~4cm deep, no fan noise in a sealed space. The stand frames are also no longer needed as lighting is integrated into the enclosure fins.
Removed — replaced by mini PC
Acer Aspire 5 laptop — Intel i5, 8GB RAM, Win 11
Previously recommended as the booth computer
S$700–800
Why replaced: A laptop is ~38×26×2cm and requires approximately 25–30cm of internal vertical clearance. The Beelink SER5 Pro mini PC is 12×12×5cm — a fraction of the size — and runs LumaBooth for Windows identically. Replacing the laptop with a mini PC directly saved 25cm of tower height, allowing the enclosure to be 145cm rather than 170cm+. The Beelink also draws less power, generates less heat, and costs S$420–520 less. Operator interaction is via a compact Bluetooth keyboard that stores inside the operator side panel.
