Design summary
What changed and why
This version resolves every issue raised during planning — height, the light arm eyesore, laptop access, light coverage at 1.5m, and fin transport.
Height reduced
185 → 145cm
Camera at 140cm — more flattering upward angle for groups. Mini PC replaces laptop to save 25cm of internal height.
Lights enclosed
Arms → fins
Protruding Godox arms replaced with 45cm aluminium wing fins. LED panels recessed behind frosted polycarbonate. No exposed hardware.
Transport solved
Hinged fold
45cm fins fold flat against the tower on 180° hinges with a stop tab at 45° inward. Fits in Hiace van. 55cm fins require detachable pin system.
Laptop access resolved
Three options designed: operator side panel at waist height, pull-out drawer shelf, or external laptop behind the tower. Mini PC + wireless keyboard recommended for compact builds. All allow mid-event access without opening the full rear door.
Light coverage confirmed
45cm fins at 30–35° inward angle with 60–80W bicolour LED panels provides 120–150cm coverage at 1.5m — sufficient for groups of 2–4. Operator-accessible brightness and CCT knobs recessed in the tower side.

Wing fin system
Fin design, hinge and transport
The definitive specification after testing fin spread vs. coverage and transport constraints.
Final spec: 45cm fins · single-knuckle 180° hinge · 30–35° inward stop tab · folds flat for Hiace transport. Do NOT use 55cm fins with this hinge — at 55cm use detachable pin system instead.
Recommended
45cm fins — hinged fold
Single-knuckle 180° piano hinge with welded stop tab at 30–35° inward
Fin length45 cm each side
Deployed width134 cm total
Packed width44 cm (tower only)
Hinge type180° piano hinge, full-length
Stop tab angle30–35° inward (welded)
Setup time~30 sec — fold out, click stop

The stop tab is a steel block welded inside the hinge at 30–35°. When the fin opens to that angle it hits the stop and cannot open further — no bolts, no tools, no fiddling at the venue. Fold it out until it clicks. Done.

Lighting system
LED panels, coverage and controls
Flat bicolour LED panels replace the Godox SL60W softboxes. External operator controls for brightness and CCT.
Recommended panels
Viltrox VL-60B or Godox LiteMax 60 — bicolour flat LED panel × 2
60W · 3200–5600K · ~4cm deep · fits inside 8cm fin cavity
Power60W per panel
CCT range3200K–5600K (bicolour)
CRI95+ — skin tones look natural
Depth~4 cm — fits 8cm fin cavity easily
Panel size~30 × 20 cm (fits 45cm fin width)
Price in SGS$120–180 each · Shopee / Lazada
Bicolour is the key spec — one panel covers the full 3200K–5600K range. No separate warm and cool panels needed. The built-in CCT and dimmer circuit can be driven by external rotary encoders wired in parallel — this is how the operator knobs on the tower side work.
Why the original Godox SL60W softboxes cannot be used
The SL60W is a cylindrical lamp head with a Bowens mount — it requires a 25cm-deep cavity minimum when paired with a softbox. The fin cavity is 8cm deep. Additionally the SL60W has an active cooling fan that exhausts heat rearward — this fan would be blocked inside a sealed fin. Flat LED panels are the correct replacement: same light quality, 4cm deep, passive or low-noise cooling.

External operator control system
Two recessed rotary knobs on the right face of the tower at ~110cm height. One controls brightness (PWM dimmer), one controls CCT. Both are flush-mounted — no protrusion — and operate both panels simultaneously via a parallel wiring run through the hinge cables.
Hardware needed: 2× rotary encoder with push-button (Shopee, ~S$5 each), 1× PWM LED dimmer controller (~S$15–20), 1× bicolour CCT controller (~S$20–30). Total ~S$45–60 in control hardware. Fabricator cuts the 22mm apertures; you wire the controllers into the circuit.
At events: Turn CCT warm for birthday/wedding vibes, cool for corporate. Reduce brightness if the venue already has strong ambient light to avoid washed-out prints. Takes 5 seconds to adjust between sessions.
Cable routing: LED panel power and control signals pass through the piano hinge via a spiral-wrapped 2-core cable. Use 1.5mm² cable minimum for the power run. When fin is folded, cable loops loosely inside the hinge — no strain on connectors.
Operator access
Laptop / mini PC access
The original sealed design was identified as a problem. Three solutions — choose based on your preference.
Why access matters mid-event: rebooting LumaBooth if it freezes, switching session modes, checking WiFi, clearing storage, responding to Windows pop-ups, swapping SD cards, connecting USB drives for overlay templates.
Recommended
Option A — operator side panel
A dedicated lockable panel on the right face of the tower at waist height (80–140cm) exposes just the mini PC shelf. Push-to-open magnetic latch — no handle visible. Guests never see it from the front. You reach in, adjust, close in seconds.
Add-on cost: S$80–150
Good ergonomics
Option B — pull-out drawer shelf
Mini PC sits on a slide-out drawer that pulls forward from the rear of the tower on drawer runners. Pull it out to access, push it back when done. Most ergonomic solution. Fabricators build these for equipment consoles regularly.
Add-on cost: S$150–200
Simplest
Option C — mini PC external
Mini PC sits on a small shelf behind the tower, not inside it. All cables run through a managed channel into the tower base. Always accessible. Simplest to build — nothing extra to fabricate. Mini PC is hidden behind the unit.
Add-on cost: S$0 (simpler build)
Why a mini PC instead of a laptop
Height saving: A laptop takes up 25–30cm of internal vertical space. A Beelink SER mini PC or Intel NUC is 12×12×5cm — saves significant height and lets the tower stay at 145cm rather than requiring 170cm+.
Recommended models: Beelink SER5 Pro (AMD Ryzen 5 5600H, 16GB RAM, ~S$280 on Lazada) or Beelink EQ12 (Intel N100, 8GB RAM, ~S$180). Both run LumaBooth flawlessly. Windows 11 Pro pre-installed.
Operator interface: Pair with a compact Bluetooth keyboard-trackpad combo (~S$40 on Shopee) that hangs on a hook inside the rear door or operator panel. You only need it for initial setup and mid-event adjustments — not continuously.
Display output: The mini PC drives the ASUS touchscreen via HDMI + USB-A (for touch data). The operator side panel does not need a screen — you check the touchscreen for feedback or use a phone with Booth CoPilot remote app for most adjustments.
Boot and update discipline: Disable Windows automatic updates before every event. Set the mini PC to boot LumaBooth on startup. This means power-on at the venue → LumaBooth ready in ~45 seconds → no operator intervention needed.

How to commission the build
Step-by-step build process
From first measurement to first event. Allow 4–8 weeks total.
1
Do 10–15 paid events first
Non-negotiable. Real setup experience tells you exactly where screen height feels wrong, where the print slot needs to be, what needs faster access. Building too early permanently locks in mistakes.
2
Physically measure every component
DNP printer (32×35×28cm), ASUS VT168HR bezel, Canon R50 lens diameter (~65mm), Beelink mini PC (~12×12×5cm), Viltrox VL-60B panel (~30×20×4cm). Add 10–15mm clearance all sides. No fabricator can work from estimates.
3
Create concept sketch and reference board
Draw front, side, and back views — even by hand. Mark all component positions and heights. Collect 5–10 reference images of premium photobooth enclosures or kiosks on Pinterest. This is what you bring to the fabricator consultation to eliminate ambiguity.
4
Commission DXF/DWG technical drawings
Hire a freelance CAD draughtsperson on Upwork or Fiverr (S$80–200). Convert sketch + measurements into dimensioned 2D drawings. This is what the fabricator cuts from. Allow 1 week for 2–5 revision rounds. Do not skip.
5
Get written quotes from 2–3 fabricators
Bring CAD drawings and reference images to Sunny Metal, Anderco, and one other. Ask for: material/thickness recommendation, timeline, finish options, revision process, and whether a bare-metal fit check before powder coating is included. Get itemised written quotes — fabrication, finishing, delivery, separately.
6
Confirm RAL colour and request a powder coat swatch
Choose your RAL number before signing off. Recommended choices: RAL 9005 (matte black), RAL 9003 (signal white), RAL 9006 (white aluminium metallic), RAL 7021 (black grey). Request a physical sample panel from Sunny Metal — colours differ significantly on screen vs real metal.
7
Bare-metal fit check before any finishing
Ask fabricator to assemble the unpainted shell and let you mount all equipment before powder coating. Verify screen position, camera height, print slot alignment, fin hinge angle, and operator panel clearance. Fix anything needed, then approve for finishing. This step costs nothing extra if agreed upfront.
8
Internal fit-out — do this yourself
Once finished shell is delivered: install VESA monitor bracket, printer shelf with rubber mounts, mini PC shelf, LED panel wiring through hinge cables, internal 4-gang power strip, cable management channels (velcro ties, not zip ties), CCT and dimmer control knobs. Budget a weekend. Test everything before booking an event.
9
Branding and full dry-run test
Apply logo vinyl decal or laser-engraved panel. Make an A3 insert for the graphic slot. Then do a complete dry-run at home — full setup, run LumaBooth, fire the printer 20+ times, test QR sharing, fold and unfold fins 5 times, open every panel. Do not take it to a paid event until this passes twice.

Critical guidance
Practical tips
Everything learned from the full planning conversation — the things that will save you from expensive mistakes.
Before you build
Do not build the enclosure until after 10–15 paid events. After real events you will know exactly where the screen height feels wrong, where the print slot should be, and what needs quicker access mid-event. Building too early permanently locks mistakes into metal.
Do a bare-metal fit check before powder coating. Always ask the fabricator to assemble the raw shell and let you mount all equipment before any finishing. Discovering a 3mm misalignment after painting is expensive. It costs nothing extra if agreed upfront.
Budget 4–8 weeks total from first fabricator contact to finished unit. Allow for 2–3 revision rounds, the fit check, powder coat lead time, and your own fit-out work. Do not commit the enclosure to a specific event booking until you have dry-run tested it twice.
Design decisions
45cm fins with hinged fold is the right call. 55cm gives slightly more coverage but requires a detachable pin system, two extra pieces to carry per event, and 90 seconds per fin to attach. 45cm with 30–35° angle gives equivalent practical coverage for groups of 2–4 and folds flat in under 5 seconds.
The operator side panel is worth the extra S$80–150. Mid-event laptop access is not hypothetical — you will need it. A full-height rear door is for emergencies; the operator panel is for routine adjustments. Both are needed.
The A3 graphic insert slot is a legitimate revenue stream. Charge S$30–50 per event for a custom-printed insert with the client's monogram or logo. Your print cost is under S$5. Clients perceive enormous value from seeing their branding on the booth itself.
Ventilation perforations at the printer section are non-negotiable. The DNP DS-RX1HS generates significant heat during back-to-back printing. Without ventilation the printer throttles within 45 minutes inside a sealed metal box. Laser-perforated panels look premium and solve this entirely.
Transport and events
The fully loaded enclosure will weigh 50–65 kg. The empty metal shell travels separately from the equipment. Carry the shell into position at the venue, then load equipment inside. Never attempt to move the fully loaded tower up stairs or through narrow corridors.
Allow 90 minutes for setup. Fins (5 min) → backdrop (10 min) → tower positioning (5 min) → internal connections (15 min) → software boot + test print (15 min) → QR test (10 min) → props + final check (10 min) → 25 min buffer. Arriving 45 minutes before the event starts is too late.
Confirm loading bay and lift before every booking. The enclosure cannot go up stairs alone. Ask when confirming: is the function room ground floor? Is there a goods lift? Many hotel ballrooms and community club halls are not ground level.
Post the enclosure on Instagram and TikTok immediately. A 30-second Reel of the enclosure being set up and guests using it generates higher-value enquiries faster than any other marketing you can do. The visual difference from a bare tripod setup is dramatic. This is how you justify charging S$180–220/hr.
